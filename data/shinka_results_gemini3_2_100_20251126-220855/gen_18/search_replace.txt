<DESCRIPTION>
Introduces randomized victim selection to the Adaptive Replacement Cache (ARC) implementation. Instead of strictly evicting the Least Recently Used (LRU) item, the algorithm selects a random victim from the bottom K (default 5) items of the target list (T1 or T2). This randomization helps to desynchronize the cache eviction from worst-case cyclic scan patterns that are slightly larger than the cache capacity, reducing thrashing while preserving the general benefits of ARC's Recency/Frequency balance.
</DESCRIPTION>
